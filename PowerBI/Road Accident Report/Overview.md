
# ROAD ACCIDENT CASUALTY ANALYSIS

## 📌 Overview
This project explores road accident data to uncover patterns in casualty severity, vehicle involvement, and environmental conditions. The goal is to better understand the key factors contributing to road incidents and identify opportunities to improve traffic safety.
An interactive Power BI dashboard was developed to transform raw accident data into actionable insights, enabling stakeholders to monitor trends, assess risk factors, and support data-driven decision-making in road safety management.

## 🎯 Objectives
-	Monitor total casualties and identify trends over time
-	Analyze accident severity distribution (fatal, serious, slight)
-	Evaluate the impact of different vehicle types on casualty rates
-	Assess how road type and road conditions influence accidents
-	Explore environmental factors such as light and location (urban vs rural)
## 📊 Dataset
-	**Source:** Public road accident dataset (open-source)
-	**Size:** 307,973
-	**Features:** Accident severity, vehicle type, road type, road condition, light condition, location, and date
-	**Timeframe:** 2021 – 2022
## 📈 Key Metrics (KPIs)
-	**Total Casualties:** 417,883
-	**Fatal Casualties:** 7,135 (1.7%)
-	**Serious Casualties:** 59,312 (14.2%)
-	**Slight Casualties:** 351,436 (84.1%)
-	**Car-Related Casualties:** 333,485 (79.8%)

## 🔍 Key Insights
-	**Low-severity accidents dominate,** with slight casualties accounting for over 84% of total incidents, indicating a high frequency of less severe but widespread accidents.
-	**Cars are the primary contributors** to road casualties, responsible for nearly 80% of all cases, highlighting the need for targeted safety measures for passenger vehicles.
-	**Single carriageway roads pose the highest risk,** contributing the largest share of casualties compared to other road types.
-	Accidents occur more frequently under **dry road conditions,** suggesting that traffic volume plays a larger role than adverse weather in accident occurrence.
-	**Urban areas experience significantly higher casualty volumes** than rural areas, likely due to denser traffic and increased road usage.
-	**Daytime driving presents greater risk exposure,** with substantially more accidents recorded compared to nighttime conditions.
-	Monthly trends show **higher casualty counts in late months (October–November),** followed by a decline in December 2022.

## 🛠 Tools & Skills
-	**Power BI:** Data visualization & dashboard design
-	**Data Modeling:** Structuring relationships and calculated measures
-	**DAX:** KPI calculations and aggregations
-	**Data Analysis:** Trend identification and insight generation

## 🚀 Impact
This dashboard enables stakeholders such as traffic authorities and urban planners to:
-	Identify high-risk road conditions and locations
-	Allocate resources more effectively for accident prevention
-	Develop targeted safety campaigns based on vehicle and environment risk factors
-	Monitor trends to evaluate the effectiveness of road safety policies
